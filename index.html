Overview

Welcome to Coding Over College.

The overarching chapter tabs—Data Structures, Named Algorithms, Python Libraries, and more—are divided into subcategories, each containing topics relevant to their respective chapters. The goal of this project is to provide a beginner-friendly introduction to advanced topics, offering not only enough to get you started but also enough to keep you going. This project has been a long time in the making. I derived much of my inspiration from W3Schools, and I highly recommend checking them out if you haven't already. They are my top reference, listed under the References tab.

Inspiration

As I sit in my Data Analytics with Python class, I watch my teacher attempt to cover the entire Python language in a two-hour lecture. I can't help but think—there must be a better way to learn alongside him. That thought led me to hatch a plan: to create a website that simplifies complex jargon in the field while serving as a hub for libraries, algorithms, and data structures, ensuring that developers don’t have to reinvent the wheel. My goal is not only to provide a resource for beginners who want a deeper understanding of Python and computer science as a whole but also to refine my own knowledge in the process. I firmly believe that you don’t truly understand something until you can explain a high-level concept to a fifth grader. My hope is that anyone reading this develops the same passion for the craft that I hold myself. In a time of growing uncertainty in the field, I want to personally thank those who take the time to learn. Don’t be discouraged—you can learn.
